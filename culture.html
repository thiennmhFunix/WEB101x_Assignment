About Culture

Dong Thap has, since December 22, 2008, the largest Buddha statue of the Mekong Delta, built in Quan Am Pagoda: "The sculpture, 32 meters long and weighing 100 tons, is that of Buddha lying on his side on a lotus petal, his right arm supporting his head and his left arm lying along his body. Buddhist scriptures say the Buddha assumed this position when he attained Nirvana, the release from the cycle of death and rebirth of an enlightened person.

Dong Thap also has a long-term history with different cultural and historical relics. Traditional trade villages is one of the strengths to developing tourism in Dong Thap.

When coming to some specific temple, you could be really impressed by different kind of giant lotus!

Also, as a immense nature land, there is a diversity kind of creatures. Some of them are in danger that needs to be protected and reserved!

People here has a traditional culture of planting flowers. There is also a big village where people have been planting many kind of flowers.

Visitors could easily come and explore special plants & creatures because they are everywhere!
